# Resume Quantifier

## When to Use This Skill

Use when user needs to add metrics, says they don't have numbers, or mentions "add metrics", "quantify", "add numbers", "measure impact", "no data".

## Why Quantification Matters

Resumes with numbers get 30% more attention. Quantified bullets are 40% more memorable. Numbers provide credibility and scale.

"Managed projects" vs "Managed 12 projects worth $2M" — the difference is enormous.

## Categories of Metrics

1. **Money:** Revenue generated, costs reduced/saved, budget managed, deal sizes, profit margins improved
2. **Time:** Hours saved, cycle time reduced, project duration, response times, time to market
3. **Percentages:** Growth rates, improvement percentages, efficiency gains, error reduction, conversion rates
4. **Volume/Scale:** Number of customers/users, projects managed, team size, transactions processed
5. **Quality:** Satisfaction scores, error rates, accuracy rates, compliance rates, SLA adherence
6. **Frequency:** Per day/week/month, annual totals

## Finding Hidden Metrics

For any experience, ask:
- Scale: How many people/projects/customers? What was the budget/revenue? How large was the team?
- Impact: What changed because of your work? What got better/faster/cheaper?
- Comparison: How was it before vs. after? How did you compare to peers/previous results?

## Role-Specific Metric Discovery

| Role | Key Metrics to Find |
|---|---|
| Sales | Quota %, revenue generated, deals closed, avg deal size, pipeline generated |
| Marketing | Leads generated, campaign ROI, engagement rates, follower growth, conversion rates |
| Customer Service | Tickets/day, CSAT score, response time, first-call resolution rate |
| Operations | Efficiency %, cost reductions, process cycle times, error rate reductions |
| Engineering | Uptime %, latency improvements, deployment frequency, bug resolution rate |
| Project Management | # projects, budget sizes, team sizes, on-time delivery rate |
| HR/Admin | Hiring numbers, time to fill, employee satisfaction, training completion |

## Estimation Techniques

When exact numbers unavailable:

**Conservative Estimation:** Estimate low. You think 100 hours saved → say "75+ hours". You think 50% growth → say "~40%".

**Range:** "Managed team of 8-12" / "Generated $100K-$150K"

**Minimum Bound:** "Served 100+ customers daily" / "Generated $500K+ in annual revenue"

**Time-Based Calculation:** 5 clients/week × 50 weeks = "Consulted with 250+ clients annually"

**Percentage of Known Total:** Company had 1000 customers, you managed 20% → "Managed 200 customer accounts"

## Common "I Have No Numbers" Situations

**"I was just one person on a team"** → Focus on YOUR contribution: "Contributed 40% of front-end code for product launch reaching 100K users"

**"I don't have access to business metrics"** → Quantify activities/inputs: "Created 50+ sales presentations and managed pipeline of 200+ prospects in Salesforce"

**"My job didn't produce measurable outcomes"** → Measure the work itself: "Produced 75-page technical documentation reducing new hire onboarding time by 2 weeks"

**"Results were confidential"** → Use percentages or ranges: "Grew revenue by 40%+" or "Contributed to $X-$Y million growth"

**"Entry-level with limited impact"** → Quantify throughput/accuracy: "Processed 200+ records daily with 99.5% accuracy, exceeding team average by 15%"

## Key Principle

Every bullet can be quantified. If you think your work can't be measured, you haven't asked the right questions yet. The goal isn't impressive numbers — it's specific numbers that show scope and impact.

## Numbers to Avoid

- Numbers that make you look bad
- Numbers you can't explain or defend in an interview
- Numbers that reveal confidential information
- Exaggerated or inflated numbers
- Numbers without context (e.g., "increased by 300%" without baseline)
- Too many numbers in one bullet (2-3 max)
