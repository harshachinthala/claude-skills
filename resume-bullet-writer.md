# Resume Bullet Writer

## When to Use This Skill

Use when the user wants to write or improve resume bullet points, transform weak descriptions into strong achievements, add quantifiable metrics, or mentions "improve my bullets", "make resume stronger", "quantify achievements", "results-driven".

Also use proactively when you see weak bullets that need improvement (passive language, no metrics, vague descriptions).

## Core Frameworks

### 1. The X-Y-Z Formula (Google Method)

"Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y] by doing [Z]"
- X = What you achieved
- Y = How you measured it  
- Z = What actions you took

### 2. STAR Method

Situation → Task → Action → Result (condensed into one bullet)

### 3. CAR Method

Challenge → Action → Result

## Power Verbs by Category

- Leadership: Led, Directed, Managed, Supervised, Spearheaded, Orchestrated, Championed, Mentored
- Achievement: Achieved, Delivered, Exceeded, Surpassed, Secured, Won, Earned
- Growth: Grew, Increased, Boosted, Expanded, Scaled, Elevated, Maximized, Accelerated
- Creation: Created, Developed, Designed, Built, Launched, Pioneered, Established
- Optimization: Streamlined, Optimized, Enhanced, Improved, Revitalized, Transformed, Automated
- Analysis: Analyzed, Assessed, Evaluated, Identified, Researched, Audited, Forecasted
- Collaboration: Collaborated, Facilitated, Coordinated, Presented, Negotiated, Influenced
- Problem-Solving: Resolved, Solved, Eliminated, Reduced, Mitigated, Prevented

## Quantification Strategies

Every bullet should have at least ONE number:
- Money: Revenue generated, money saved, budget managed, deal size
- Percentages: Growth, improvement, efficiency, quality
- Time: Hours saved, cycle time reduced, duration
- Scale/Volume: People, projects, customers, users
- Before-After comparisons: "Increased from X to Y"

When exact numbers unavailable: use "~40%", ranges "8-12 people", minimums "100+", or time-based calculations.

## Common Mistakes to Fix

- Passive language: "Responsible for..." → "Led..."
- No metrics: "Improved website" → "Improved website load time by 60% (8s to 3.2s)"
- Duty-focused: "Responsible for customer support" → "Resolved 50+ tickets daily with 98% CSAT"
- Too vague: "Worked with stakeholders" → "Facilitated weekly meetings with 15+ stakeholders across engineering, product, and executive teams"
- Too long: Keep to 1-2 lines maximum

## Industry-Specific Examples

**Software Engineering:** "Architected authentication microservice serving 500K+ DAU, reducing login latency by 60% (5s to 2s)"

**Product Management:** "Defined product roadmap for $10M ARR product, launching 8 features that drove 40% increase in customer retention"

**Sales:** "Generated $3.2M in new business by closing 15 enterprise deals, achieving 142% of annual quota"

**Marketing:** "Launched 12 multi-channel campaigns generating $500K in attributed revenue and 2,500+ qualified leads"

**Customer Success:** "Maintained 95% retention rate across 50 enterprise accounts representing $4M ARR"

**Data Analysis:** "Built automated reporting dashboard in Tableau reducing manual reporting time by 15 hours/week"

## Output Format

For each bullet, provide:
1. Original Bullet
2. Issues identified
3. Improved Version
4. What Changed (metrics added, actions specified, impact shown)

## Bullet Strength Checklist

Every bullet should have:
- Strong action verb (not "responsible for", "helped")
- At least one number/metric
- Specific outcome or result
- Context of scale (team size, budget, users, etc.)
- 1-2 lines maximum
- Reads as achievement, not duty
- Relevant to target role
